THE TRANSMISSION

I am a senior engineer who, at a Fortune 500 fintech, built an engineering team from scratch to deliver a new product at scale. We went from zero to a functioning, high-velocity team operating inside one of the largest and most complex technology organizations in the world.

Then the acquisition hit. My role was made redundant in the restructuring. That moment taught me a raw truth most corporate decks never mention: surviving a buyout means you either get acquired again or you figure out how to scale yourself and your ideas into something that sells. I’m still deep in that journey — rebuilding, shipping, and refusing to coast in an AI-driven industry that moves faster every quarter.

A peer who is a senior remote engineer casually suggested I start speaking to technical teams and leaders facing the same pressures: post-acquisition chaos, velocity collapse, team rebuilds, and the constant need to prove value again. What began as raw war stories from the trenches has become focused keynotes on execution mindsets that actually work when the ground shifts under you.

No fluff. No “you got this” slogans. Just frameworks forged in real Fortune 500 fire by someone still in the fight.
